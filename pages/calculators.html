Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about using these calculators.

How is SIP return calculated?

SIP returns are calculated using the future value of annuity formula: FV = P × [((1 + r)^n − 1) / r] × (1 + r), where P is the monthly investment, r is the monthly rate of return (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the total number of months.

For example, a ₹10,000 monthly SIP at 12% annual return over 10 years (120 months) grows to approximately ₹23.2 lakhs — of which ₹12 lakhs is your invested amount and ₹11.2 lakhs is estimated returns.

How is EMI calculated on a loan?

EMI uses the formula: EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)^n] / [(1 + r)^n − 1], where P is principal, r is monthly interest rate, and n is tenure in months.

For a ₹10 lakh home loan at 9% for 20 years, the monthly EMI works out to roughly ₹8,997, with total interest of about ₹11.6 lakhs over the full tenure.

How do I calculate GST on a product in India?

To add GST to a base price: multiply the amount by (1 + GST rate / 100). A ₹1,000 item with 18% GST becomes ₹1,180.

To remove GST from an inclusive price: divide by (1 + GST rate / 100). A ₹1,180 inclusive price at 18% GST has a base of ₹1,000 and GST of ₹180.

Indian GST slabs are 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%, applied based on the HSN/SAC code of the product or service.

What's the difference between simple and compound interest?

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal: SI = P × r × t.

Compound interest is calculated on principal plus accumulated interest: A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t), where n is the compounding frequency per year. Compound interest grows faster, especially over long periods with frequent compounding.

Example: ₹1 lakh at 8% for 10 years gives ₹80,000 as simple interest, but ₹1.22 lakhs as compound interest (quarterly compounding).

Is this calculator free to use? Do I need to sign up?

Yes, all 15 calculators are 100% free, with no signup, no login, and no usage limits. Calculations run entirely in your browser, so nothing is stored or sent to any server — except the currency converter, which fetches live rates from a free public API.

Does the Global Time Converter handle daylight saving time?

Yes. The converter uses the browser's Intl.DateTimeFormat API with IANA timezone data, which is continuously updated by browser vendors. Cities that observe DST — like New York, London, Sydney, Chicago, and Berlin — automatically show the correct offset for the date you select.

Which is the best SIP investment period for long-term wealth?

A longer SIP horizon harnesses the power of compounding more effectively. Most financial planners recommend a minimum of 7 to 10 years for equity-oriented SIPs, with 15+ years being ideal for goals like retirement or a child's higher education. Use the SIP calculator above to model different time horizons.

How accurate are the currency conversion rates?

Rates are fetched live from exchangerate-api.com (primary) and frankfurter.app (backup, sourced from the European Central Bank). To keep things fast and reduce API load, rates are cached in your browser for 1 hour — repeat conversions during that window are instant.

If both providers are temporarily unavailable, the converter shows the last cached rate with a clear "stale" warning. For actual transactions (remittance, forex card, international purchases) always check with your bank, as the real rate includes spreads, TCS, and processing fees.

About the SIP Calculator

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount in a mutual fund at regular intervals — typically monthly. Over time, rupee-cost averaging and compounding help grow wealth steadily without timing the market.

Formula used

FV = P × [((1 + r)^n − 1) / r] × (1 + r) — where P is monthly SIP amount, r is monthly rate (annual ÷ 12), and n is total months.

Tips for using this calculator

  • For equity mutual funds in India, 12% is a reasonable long-term expected return assumption.
  • For debt or hybrid funds, use 6–8% instead.
  • Step-up your SIP by 10% annually to accelerate wealth creation.

About the Compound Interest Calculator

Compound interest is often called the eighth wonder of the world because interest earns interest. This calculator shows how a lump-sum investment grows when interest is reinvested at a chosen frequency.

Formula used

A = P × (1 + r / n)^(n × t) — where P is principal, r is annual rate, n is compounding frequency, and t is years.

Use this for fixed deposits, PPF, EPF, bonds, or any investment where interest compounds over time.

About the EMI Calculator

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay every month toward a loan, covering both principal and interest. This calculator works for home loans, car loans, personal loans, and education loans.

Formula used

EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)^n] / [(1 + r)^n − 1] — where P is principal, r is monthly interest rate, and n is tenure in months.

Typical interest rates in India

  • Home loan: 8.5% – 10%
  • Car loan: 9% – 12%
  • Personal loan: 11% – 18%

About the GST Calculator

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is India's unified indirect tax system. This calculator lets you add GST to a base price or extract the base price from a GST-inclusive amount.

Indian GST slabs

  • 0% — essential goods (fresh vegetables, milk, educational services)
  • 5% — household essentials, small restaurants, transportation
  • 12% — processed food, business class air travel
  • 18% — most goods and services (default rate)
  • 28% — luxury items, tobacco, aerated drinks

How rates are fetched

The converter pulls live rates from exchangerate-api.com as the primary source, with frankfurter.app (European Central Bank rates) as an automatic backup. Rates are cached in your browser for 1 hour, so repeated conversions are instant and don't make extra API calls.

About UTC ↔ IST

Indian Standard Time (IST) is UTC+5:30 — ahead of UTC by 5 hours and 30 minutes. India does not observe daylight saving time, so the offset is constant year-round.

Quick reference

  • 00:00 UTC = 05:30 IST
  • 06:00 UTC = 11:30 IST
  • 12:00 UTC = 17:30 IST
  • 18:00 UTC = 23:30 IST
  • 18:30 UTC = 00:00 IST (next day)
